Strategy Game

Fortunately or unfortunately, college admissions is in many ways a strategy game. Although other factors such as luck and natural ability are involved, approaching the process strategically will lead to the best possible outcome. Take something like chess. A chess grandmaster will always be able to force his way through a lower ranked novice player. However, someone with no knowledge of chess would find beating anyone an insurmountable challenge. For us, as people who have thoroughly navigated this system and played the game, we believe that we have the playbook that will help our clients reach their maximum potential.

There are three factors that are considered by admissions officers: high school coursework and standardized test scores, extracurricular activities, and the college admissions application itself, including the all important essays. With that being said, this is our philosophy of the process:

1. Identifying “target” schools and following a set schedule in the application process is vital:

For many parents and students, the timeline of applying for schools, the jargon involved in the application process, the difficulty in pinpointing schools to apply to, and the overwhelming urgency of the situation often makes the college application season far more stressful and difficult than it needs to be. Nov. 1st, Dec. 15th, Jan 1st, REA, RD, ED. These are all buzzwords that can be difficult to keep track of and can otherwise ruin a would-be successful college application season. We will work closely with your child to ensure a well tailored list of colleges and deadlines that make sense and ensure success.

2. A well crafted application is crucial:

For many elite universities, low GPA and standardized test scores can significantly reduce likelihood for admission. That being said, the threshold for GPA/standardized testing is fairly large. The college admissions application can provide the opportunity for a well formed account of a student's successes and failures, which if well-formed can overcome a large spectrum of GPA and standardized testing outcomes. This is where we come in: understanding the purpose of the application and how to highlight strengths in a way that overcome certain weaknesses is critical. We will offer guidance on all aspects of the application, from crafting the extracurricular section, to the common application essay and supplemental school specific application essay.

3. Developing your true passions in an organic way is the best way to ensure college success and beyond:

By starting our mentoring early in the application process, we will help your child identify the activities that are most aligned with their passions and help them carve their own stories for themselves. Above all, college application readers keep in mind one notion: intellectual vitality. This is difficult to fake, and even when faked successfully can lead to adverse consequences. Students who are led to the finish line for the sake of college application success often find it harder to identify their goals in college which can lead to a lack of job market success and mental health difficulties. Our model is different—we will nurture your child’s honest passions and find practical ways for them to develop them while building a resume for college application success.

MentorPrep is trying to demystify the process of college admissions. As two students who have recently taken part in the college admissions process and been very successful in gaining admittance into many elite universities, we can help your child develop their passions into college success.
